Golden Retriever

The Golden Retriever is a large-sized breed of dog bred as gun dogs to retrieve shot waterfowl such as ducks and upland game birds during hunting and shooting parties, and were named 'retriever' because of their ability to retrieve shot game undamaged.

  • Scientific name: Canis lupus familiaris
  • Color: Any shades of gold or cream
  • Life Span: Average 10-12 years
  • Origin: Scotland
  • Temperament: Confident, Friendly, Intelligent, Kind, Reliable, Trustworthy

Although most dogs are loyal, especially if you take good care of them, some can still be independent and aloof. This is what makes the Golden Retriever special. Unlike other breeds that would rather be dominant, they enjoy being commanded by their owners. Best of all, they pay importance to family time, making them the perfect companion and pet for those with children or already starting a family.
